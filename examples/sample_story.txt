The Littlest Star

High above the rooftops, past the clouds and past the moon, lived the littlest star in the whole night sky. Her name was Pip.

Every night, the big stars shone bright and proud. But Pip's light was small, like a candle behind a curtain. "One day," Pip whispered, "I will find someone who needs a little light."

Down below, in a small house with a blue door, a bunny named Bo could not sleep. His room was too dark, and the dark felt too big.

Bo looked out the window and sighed a very small sigh. Pip heard it. Stars are good at hearing small sighs.

So Pip floated down, soft as a snowflake, and sat on Bo's windowsill. Her little light filled the room like warm milk fills a cup. Not too bright. Not too dark. Just right.

"Hello," said Bo. "Are you lost?"

"No," said Pip. "I am found. You needed a little light, and a little light is just what I have."

Bo snuggled under his blanket. Pip hummed a tiny star song. Around and around the night sky turned, slow and gentle, like a lullaby.

Bo's eyes grew heavy. The dark did not feel big anymore. It felt like a soft blue blanket over the world.

"Goodnight, Pip," yawned Bo.

"Goodnight, Bo," twinkled Pip. And she glowed, small and steady, all night long.
