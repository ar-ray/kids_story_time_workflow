Title: Toby the Turtle Who Wanted to Fly
Toby the turtle spent every morning watching the swallows swoop and dive above the pond. "If only I could fly," he sighed, "then I'd really be something special."
One day, Toby begged two geese to help him. They held a stick between their beaks, Toby bit down on the middle, and up they went! The pond shrank below him. The wind rushed past his shell. Toby was flying!
But as they soared over the meadow, a rabbit shouted, "Look! A flying turtle! How clever those geese are!" Toby wanted to yell, "It was MY idea!" — and the moment he opened his mouth, he let go of the stick.
Down, down, down he tumbled... SPLASH! Right into the deep end of the pond. As he sank, something wonderful happened. Toby glided through the cool water, twisting and turning, faster and more graceful than any swallow in the sky. A school of minnows watched in awe. "Look how he swims!" they bubbled. "How does he DO that?"
Toby smiled all the way to the surface. The swallows could keep the sky. He had the whole pond — and down here, he was the one who could fly.
Moral: Instead of wishing for someone else's gifts, discover the magic in your own.
